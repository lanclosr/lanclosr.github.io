Flooding in Houston: Know Your Risk

By Ryan Lanclos | August 2018


Home to Houston, the 4th largest city in the United States, Harris County, Texas is bisected by 22 bayous with more than 2,500 miles of waterway – a unique geography that means flooding is a very real threat. In late August of 2017, Hurricane Harvey dropped an amazing 47.4 inches of rain near Clear Creek at Interstate 45. in Harris County (TX). Harvey brought a new normal to Harris County, and citizens need to prepare now for the next storm. The resources below will help you understand your flood risk.

Forming on August 17, 2017 and finally dissipating on September 2, Hurricane Harvey caused $125 billion dollars in damage to the US and was responsible for 107 deaths. In Harris County, "all 4.7 million people...were impacted – directly or indirectly – by Hurricane Harvey."

Approximately 1.4 million residents and 482,000 households are located within the current 0.2-percent-annual-chance (or 500-year) FEMA flood zone. Most of us would expect damage from Harvey to be confied to these flood zones in each watershed.

But, the new normal created by Harvey also impacted people who live outside 0.2-percent-annual-chance (or 500-year) FEMA flood zone. It impacted people who thought they were safe from flooding.The chart below shows the numbe of damaged structures inside (blue) the flood zone, and the number of structures outside (red) the flood zone for each watershed in Harris County.







If you live in the greater Houston area, you are at risk of suffering damage from flooding - even if you live outside of the 0.2-percent-annual-chance (or 500-year) FEMA flood zone.

One of the easiest, and most cost effective, ways to prepare for the new normal is by purchasing flood insurance from the National Flood Insurance Program.

Visit floodsmart.gov for more details about flood insurance.
